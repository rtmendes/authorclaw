## Genre Hook Templates

Analyze your opening against genre-specific reader expectations:

### Supported Genres
- **Thriller/Suspense** — Danger-first openings, ticking clock, in medias res
- **Romance** — Meet-cute or emotional wound, voice-heavy, chemistry signal
- **Fantasy** — World-hook (one fascinating detail), character-in-motion, magic tease
- **Mystery** — Crime or anomaly, detective intro, red herring plant
- **Sci-Fi** — World displacement, technology-as-character, status quo disruption
- **Literary Fiction** — Voice showcase, thematic seed, emotional resonance
- **Horror** — Normalcy with wrongness, dread buildup, false safety
- **YA** — Voice-first, identity conflict, immediate stakes

### What Gets Analyzed Per Genre
1. Does your opening match what readers of THIS genre expect?
2. Where do you conform vs. subvert genre conventions?
3. Comp title comparison (how do bestsellers in your genre open?)
4. "Shelf test" — Would a bookstore browser keep reading?

## Look Inside Optimizer

Amazon's "Look Inside" feature typically shows your first 250-500 words. This tool optimizes specifically for that window:

1. **Word-by-word audit** of your first 250 words
2. **Identifies** backstory dumps, passive voice, info-dumps, slow pacing
3. **Rewrites** weak sections while preserving your voice
4. **A/B comparison** — your version vs. optimized version side-by-side
5. **Conversion prediction** — estimate of "Look Inside" → purchase likelihood
